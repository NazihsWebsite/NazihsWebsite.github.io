Mystery Black Box Project Essay

The Mystery Black Box project asked us to figure out what might be inside a sealed box without opening it. Since nobody could look inside, every group built a model to represent their idea of the hidden structure. All groups used cardboard for the base and plastic materials inside the box. Our group designed a model using a funnel connected to plastic tubes and containers, hoping to show how liquid might travel through a hidden system like the one inside the real Mystery Black Box.

CLAIM–EVIDENCE–REASONING

Claim

The inside of the real Mystery Black Box might contain a system of plastic tubes and connected containers that allows liquid to move through different sections.

Evidence

Our model used a funnel that led into plastic tubes and small containers. We poured water into the funnel to test how a liquid would move through the system. Even though our model didn’t work perfectly — the tubes were attached loosely and some water spilled out — our goal was to see how fluid could travel through a hidden pathway. The water sometimes got stuck or leaked, but this helped us realize how a real system would need to be built carefully to guide liquid without making a mess. When we compared this to the real Mystery Black Box, we noticed that the real box did not make any noise. Even when we tried to listen carefully, we heard nothing. Because of this silence, we believed the inside might use a liquid system, even though our model was not perfectly sealed.

Reasoning – Part I

Models help us understand ideas even when they aren’t perfect. Our model didn’t work smoothly, but it helped us explore how a tube-and-container system might behave. We learned that liquid can travel silently if the tubes are sealed correctly, and the real Mystery Black Box staying silent supports this idea. Our mistakes showed us how much more precise the real internal system would have to be.

Reasoning – Part II

Our evidence supports the claim because a fluid system would not make the loud sounds that solid objects would. Even though our model was messy, the basic idea showed how liquid could move inside a real black box without creating noise — if the tubes were sealed properly.

Reasoning – Part III

If the opposite claim were true — that the box had solid objects — then moving or shaking it would create obvious sounds. We would hear rolling, tapping, or bouncing. Because the real Mystery Black Box made no sounds, the solid-object idea is not likely. A silent system like liquid moving through tubes makes more sense, even though our group’s model didn't function perfectly.

Photography with DSLR Cameras

I took pictures of our model using a DSLR camera to show the funnel, tubes, containers, and cardboard base. Even though the system was messy, the photos captured the structure we attempted to create.

2D Design & Animation in Adobe Photoshop

In Photoshop, I created a simple diagram showing how the water was supposed to travel through the tubes. I also made a small frame-by-frame animation that demonstrated the intended flow of water through the system, even though it didn’t work smoothly in real life. Originally, I was thinking of drawing my diagram with sensors and gears, but while talking with my group, we noticed that the Black Box is fairly quiet and would probably have made noise if it had those in it. 3D Design & Animation in Autodesk Maya

I built a cleaner version of our model in Autodesk Maya. I created the tubes and containers using many different tools on Maya, and then animated the doors flying open and using cuts got different angles. The 3D version helped show our intended design, not the messy real-life result.

21ST CENTURY SKILL REFLECTION

The skill I improved the most during this project was problem-solving. Our model didn’t work perfectly — the tubes leaked and the water didn’t flow the way we planned. But as a group, we worked together to understand what went wrong and how the design could be improved. Even though the model had issues, it helped us think through how the inside of the real Mystery Black Box might be structured.
